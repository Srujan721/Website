About

Background, education, and technical expertise

Sai Srujan Kumar Chalavadi is a researcher specializing in Rock Mechanics and Geotechnical Earthquake Engineering. He is currently serving as a Project Research Associate at the Indian Institute of Technology (IIT) Roorkee, where he recently completed his doctoral research on the seismic fragility analysis of rock tunnels in the Indian Himalayas. His work integrates advanced numerical modelling, probabilistic seismic hazard assessment, and performance-based design to evaluate the vulnerability of underground structures subjected to earthquake loading.

A recipient of the prestigious Prime Minister Research Fellowship (PMRF) and the ISRM Best Paper Award at the 13th Asian Rock Mechanics Symposium (ARMS13), he has published in leading international journals including Rock Mechanics and Rock Engineering and has presented at major conferences such as the 15th ISRM Congress in Salzburg, Austria. He is committed to open science and has made all computational tools and workflows developed during his research publicly available through GitHub. Beyond his core research, he contributes as an AI Model Trainer, leveraging his domain expertise to support the development of advanced generative AI models.

Open-Source Tools & Repositories

The computational tools, workflows, and scripts developed during this research are made publicly available to support transparency, reproducibility, and further research in seismic analysis and tunnel engineering.

Seismic_Hazard_Assessment_OpenQuake

Reproducible workflow and input files for performing probabilistic seismic hazard assessment (PSHA). Contains OpenQuake configuration files, logic-tree definitions, source model inputs, and hazard calculation scripts.

Python / OpenQuake
Seismic_Rock_Tunnel_Design

An Excel-based tunnel design tool for assessment and design of rock tunnels subjected to seismic loading. Includes the spreadsheet tool with supporting documentation on methodology and assumptions.

Excel / VBA
MATLAB-Code-for-GTS_NX

MATLAB code to estimate damage states of tunnel lining frame elements. Processes structural response outputs from nonlinear dynamic time-history analyses in MIDAS GTS NX and evaluates damage based on performance criteria.

MATLAB
